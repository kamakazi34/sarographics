## 2. Strategic model: a design house

You do not need to own manufacturing. Your edge is design plus a genuine understanding of sport. Design the kit, broker the manufacture through established Australian sublimation suppliers, and charge a design fee plus a per-unit margin. Low capital, low risk, scalable.

## 3. Best-practice operating principles (research-backed)

- **Niche hard.** Broad merch is saturated and low-margin. Win a specific community first (endurance sport, with an identity or place angle). Niche intersection beats generic.
- **Own the customer.** Etsy and Redbubble are side channels you do not control. The website and an email list are the core asset. Take winning products and scale them on owned channels.
- **Design quality is the differentiator** and the first thing a buyer judges. Protect studio time for it.
- **Sample before you sell.** Order physical samples of every product (POD and kit) before listing. Never sell what you have not held.
- **Ask for design feedback.** Sellers who skip this regret it. Use the community as a feedback loop.
- **POD is slow to compound.** Do not expect instant sales; treat it as passive backfill, not a hero stream.
- **Custom orders run on a fixed process** (see Section 6.6). Weak operations, not weak designs, sink apparel businesses.

## 6. Custom sports apparel (lead stream)

### 6.1 Decoration-method reality

- **Dye sublimation** for tri-suits, cycling and run jerseys, anything in stretch lycra. Ink fuses into the fibre: zero feel, breathable, 100+ washes, stretches without cracking. Done at manufacture.
- **DTF / heat transfer (iron-on)** for cotton and poly-cotton tees and small logo work on stable fabric. Sits on top of the fabric, cracks on high-stretch lycra, ~50 washes.

**Conclusion:** brand tri-suits and jerseys by designing the branding into the garment and sublimating at manufacture. Position iron-on as a low-volume add-on for cotton tees and logos, never for the body of a tri-suit.

### 6.5 Go-to-market

Relationship-first, not advertising-first. Target tri/cycling/running clubs, masters squads, small events, corporate charity teams. Land one lighthouse club kit, photograph it well, use it as proof for the next conversations. Lead magnet: a one-page Club Kit offer PDF.

### 6.6 The custom-order process (fixed)

1. Client sends logo, colours, requirements.
2. You send digital mockup and a written quote.
3. Client approves design **and size breakdown in writing**.
4. 30 to 50% deposit invoiced before any production.
5. Production starts at agreed lead time.
6. Balance paid before shipment.
